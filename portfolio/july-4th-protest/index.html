July 4th Protest

When & Where: Meet at UN Plaza, San Francisco, 11am


The government is using secret laws to access and store your communications. They have a secret court that is rubber stamping special warrants for access to your online communications. US phone companies are required to give up years of data and are adding more every day: information that gives them comprehensive knowledge of your activities. It’s dangerous for the government to have access to this centralized information forever.


We are going to the offices of Senator Feinstein, and to the location of the former AT&T access point in San Francisco, Room 641A, for a day to rally the public and raise awareness. This will be a peaceful demonstration. Please join us!


Route
Map http://goo.gl/maps/JjiAz
1. UN Plaza at 11am
2. Federal Building at 12pm
3. Embarcadero at 2pm
4. 611 Folsom at 3pm


Directions to UN Plaza
Public transit, Civic Center station for Muni and BART, or Caltrain at 4th and King.
From the peninsula/South Bay, take 101N to 7th street and follow the street to Market.
From East Bay, take 80W to 5th Street, take a left on Harrison and then a right on 7th street and follow the street to Market.
From North Bay, take 101S to Civic Center.
