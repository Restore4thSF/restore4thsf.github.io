We need both help with drafting and help reaching out to other groups on this. First, we need to decide what kind of resolution we want.

Text of the resolution:

Resolution in support of the right of privacy, instructing agencies of the City and County of San Francisco are not permitted to conduct or assist in mass surveillance of private communications, urging law enforcement officials to respect the right of privacy, and requesting members of Congress to both investigate past violations and prevent future violations of the Fourth Amendment of the United States Constitution.

WHEREAS Privacy is a fundamental right reflected in the Constitution of the State of California Article 1 Section 1 and protected by other state and federal law; and

WHEREAS The right to privacy confers dignity and security to residents of the City and County of San Francisco, as well as protecting their fundamental right to free speech, free association, and a free press; and

WHEREAS The Board of Supervisors passed a resolution in 2003 refusing to comply with the Patriot Act insofar as it requires violations of the rights of United States citizens; and

WHEREAS In 2006, Mark Klein exposed the National Security Agency interception of telecommunications at 611 Folsom Street in San Francisco; and

WHEREAS Recent Freedom of Information Act requests have uncovered that Federal agencies have in fact unconstitutionally violated the privacy of United States citizens using general warrants to gather domestic communications, allowing NSA analysts to use databases for personal relationships, and the confirmed storage of all metadata, indicating a policy and culture of disregard within law enforcement; and

WHEREAS The City of Oakland authorized federal surveillance of their residents using the Domain Awareness Center; and

WHEREAS Systemic violations of privacy have a chilling effect on free speech and freedom of association and prevent the transgressive views of today from becoming the progressive views of tomorrow for the betterment of mankind, and

WHEREAS The City and County of San Francisco is a city of refuge for many ideas and identities that suffer persecution, including religion, national origin, immigration status, sexuality and gender identity, economic status, mental illness, and homelessness; and

WHEREAS The City and County of San Francisco is committed being the vangard for the protection of civil rights and liberties; now therefore, be it

RESOLVED, That the City and County of San Francisco strongly affirms our commitment to the rights and liberties enshrined in the Constitution of the State of California, and the United States Constitution, including the Fourth Amendment; and, be it,

FURTHER RESOLVED, That public agencies of the City and County of San Francisco are instructed to refuse to conduct or assist in mass surveillance of private communications; and, be it,

FURTHER RESOLVED, That we ask federal and state law officials working within the City and County of San Francisco to work in accordance with local law including the right of privacy provided by the Constitution of the State of California and the United States Constitution; and, be it

FURTHER RESOLVED That the Board of Supervisors asks our Senators and members of Congress to investigate past and current unconstitutional mass surveillance and actively work to prevent the continuation of any unconstitutional surveillance, and to restore fundamental rights and liberties embodied in the Constitution of the State of California and the United States Constitution. The Board of Supervisors s hall immediately send copies of this resolution to our senators and members of Congress, the United States Select Committee on Intelligence, the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence, the US Attorney General, and the President of the United States.
