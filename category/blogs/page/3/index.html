Threat Levels

We should distinguish between different threat levels because they require different levels of encryption security. We also have to be aware that the threat level only increases if your content is “worthwhile enough” so a way to avoid needing further encryption protection would be to avoid being noticed as worthwhile. The key to that is to hide metadata.

The threat level concept eventually breaks down, however, because in order to communicate outside the secure group, you are immediately downgraded to “lowest common denominator” encryption techniques.

The City Council Project

Background
The city council project aims to have city councils in the Bay Area pass primarily non-binding resolutions condemning the mass surveillance revealed by the Snowden leaks. The purpose of these resolutions is to provide public pressure from a local level to Congressional representatives whose districts include those cities. The work of this project is primarily in drafting the resolution, reaching out to partner organizations who may want to support the resolution, and working with city councils to pass the resolution.

Encryption Tips: Email

PGP Encryption taught by Will.

Download GPG, Mozilla Thunderbird, and a plugin called Enigmail. PGP encryption was used by Glenn Greenwald and Snowden. It works with a pair of keys, a private and a public key.

Encryption is done by sequences of numbers, and you and your recipient have to share the same “cypher” for that. Simplest is Rot13, shift all the letters 13 spaces. And then you can change each letter by different increments, which creates much higher level of security. Then there’s “Asymmetric encryption” – this is our method, it uses two keys, private and public. Someone can write you an email that’s encrypted by public key and can only be decrypted with your private key. If you send an email to four people, it will be encrypted four times using their public keys.
