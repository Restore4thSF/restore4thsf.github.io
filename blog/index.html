Shareholder Activism Instruction Manual

Public companies are required by law to permit shareholders (above a threshold amount) to add items to the annual meeting’s agenda. This is largely a joke now, because shares are spread so widely that too small a number could ever attend a meeting to enact the proposal. However: due to the laws governing public companies they have to publish, read aloud, and make a show of voting on the proposal anyway.

Rick Perry is more committed to 4th Amendment Rights than Jerry Brown

Governor Jerry Brown

Today (October 12th, 2013) Governor Jerry Brown vetoed a bill that was designed to protect California residents’ privacy and freedom of thought. SB 467 requires a warrant for state and local law enforcement to demand access to the electronic communications of any person. This would include your Gmail account or Facebook message history. Here is some further background provided by the EFF. Under current law, no warrant is required for messages older than 30 days under what is known as the “third party doctrine”.
